Understanding Drugs of Abuse

Educational resource on the effects and risks of common substances

Alcohol

Alcohol is a depressant that slows down brain function and impairs thinking, coordination, and reaction time. Regular abuse can lead to addiction, liver disease, and other health issues.

Cocaine

Cocaine is a powerful stimulant that increases dopamine levels, leading to intense euphoria followed by a crash. Long-term use can damage the brain, heart, and lungs.

Nicotine

Nicotine, found in tobacco products, is highly addictive. It stimulates the release of dopamine, creating short-term pleasure but increasing the risk of heart disease and cancer.
